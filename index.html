Research Scientist · MIT CSAIL

Claudio Mayrink Verdun

Hi! I am a Research Scientist at MIT CSAIL, affiliated with MIT FutureTech, led by Neil Thompson, and the Algorithmic Alignment Group led by Dylan Hadfield-Menell. I am also a Research Associate at Harvard’s School of Public Health.

I obtained my Ph.D. in mathematics and electrical engineering (summa cum laude) from the Technical University of Munich, under the guidance of Felix Krahmer within the Optimization and Data Analysis group, while concurrently affiliated to the Information Theory group under the leadership of Holger Boche. Before joining MIT, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Harvard’s School of Engineering and Applied Sciences under the mentorship of Flavio Calmon.

My research builds rigorous mathematical foundations for trustworthy AI, drawing on optimization, information theory, and signal processing. A few threads I am most actively pursuing:

  • Inference-time alignment. Principled methods for steering AI systems at decoding time, without further training. Recent work includes Soft Best-of-n sampling and a theoretical framework for inference-time reward hacking.
  • Fairness, accountability, and provenance. Rigorous guarantees for generative systems, spanning intersectional representation in retrieval and text-to-image models, robust watermarking for LLM provenance, and formalizing how values are applied when safety rules conflict.
  • Interpretability via sparse representations. Techniques that surface the internal computation of large models through parsimony, including measuring progress in dictionary learning and, most recently, Temporal Sparse Autoencoders that leverage the sequential nature of language.

I am also interested in safeguards and cybersecurity for AI agents, the economic implications of algorithmic progress, and leveraging AI to accelerate discovery in science and mathematics. Beyond technical research, I collaborate with lawyers and policymakers on AI governance, including contributing to the G20 Summit policy discussions and developing methods to evaluate AI policy proposals at scale.
