My research builds rigorous mathematical foundations for trustworthy AI, drawing on optimization, information theory, and signal processing. A few threads I am most actively pursuing:

- *Inference-time alignment.* Principled methods for steering AI systems at decoding time, without further training. Recent work includes [Soft Best-of-n sampling](/publications/#verdun2025softbestofn) and a theoretical framework for [inference-time reward hacking](/publications/#khalaf2025inference).
- *Fairness, accountability, and provenance.* Rigorous guarantees for generative systems, spanning [intersectional representation in retrieval](/publications/#oesterling2024multi) and [text-to-image models](/publications/#jung2025multi), [robust watermarking](/publications/#tsur2025heavywater) for LLM provenance, and [formalizing how values are applied when safety rules conflict](/publications/#buyl2025ai).
- *Interpretability via sparse representations.* Techniques that surface the internal computation of large models through parsimony, including [measuring progress in dictionary learning](/publications/#karvonen2024measuring) and, most recently, [Temporal Sparse Autoencoders](/publications/#bhalla2026temporal) that leverage the sequential nature of language.

I am also interested in safeguards and cybersecurity for AI agents, the economic implications of algorithmic progress, and leveraging AI to accelerate discovery in science and mathematics. Beyond technical research, I collaborate with lawyers and policymakers on AI governance, including [contributing to the G20 Summit policy discussions](https://www.t20brasil.org/media/documentos/arquivos/TF05_ST_05_AI_TECHNOLOGIES66cdc9e290631.pdf) and developing [methods to evaluate AI policy proposals at scale](https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-9053242/v1).
